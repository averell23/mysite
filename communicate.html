Communicate!

Little has changed since I put this page up, and I decided to put things into order, and maybe to find out what this page is all about. 
The ability to communicate, by itself, is a social skill and I can hardly tell you how to do it. (Especially since I'm still working on that skills myself) 

However, the net does - as the telephone did before - enhance our possibilities to communicate. We can now send messages to the most remote places for a small charge, and we can come in contact with people we'd never had heard of otherwise. 
Exchanging letters has become faster, cheaper and easier than ever before.  
But maybe it has also become less intimate, more anonymous and impersonal.  
Also, the use of the current means of communication will be newly defined. Recieving a handwritten letter has a different meaning when your other correspondence consists only of hastily typed email messages. 

The "Internet Hype" the world is experiencing at the moment has little to do with the medium itself. The net is but a tool like many others. 
But it is a new tool, and we're still trying to find out how to use it. We are fascinated by the unknown that lies in front of us, and maybe afraid of what it may become in the end.  

We are walking this still unexplored world, gathering bricks of information, turning them, curious like children, exchanging them and wondering how they'll fit toghether to build a new kind of building. 

We need to remember that the boundaries are not out there: They are in our mind. 

We will face dangers on the way: People will try to steal our information, unwise governments, companies and others will try to walk our private places without our consent. Others will try to erect places of hate and hostility. 

It is our duty to regulate this world wisely, to allow it's inhabitants to enjoy their freedoms, and ensure that their freedoms cannot be taken away. 

This page will be an reflection of how different people define communication within this new medium. I will link to places where new ideas are discussed, and new things are tried, as well as to places where people have surrendered their curiosity to their fear. 
I will add some of my own thoughts, when I find the time. 

This selection makes no distinction between "good" or "bad". It merely offers you some starting points to roam this new world and find out what it could become.
